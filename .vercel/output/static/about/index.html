The story

Born and raised in downtown Toronto. Spent five years running short-term rentals on Airbnb — over 400 five-star guest reviews across that time. The hospitality work taught me something simple: people don't remember the listing photo. They remember whether they felt taken care of.

I transitioned to real estate full-time so I could bring that same standard to the longer, higher-stakes decisions: renting a first apartment in a new country, signing a multi-year lease, finding a tenant for a unit you depend on for income. The job is the same. The stakes are bigger.

Working philosophy

Preparation is the work

Toronto's rental market doesn't reward improvisation. It rewards the renter or landlord who showed up with their answers already in hand. Most of what I do is making sure that's my client.

Bilingual, fully

Not "I know some Chinese" bilingual — I conduct showings, review leases, explain rent control rules in fluent 中文. For Chinese-speaking newcomers, families, and investors, that's often the difference between a confident decision and a confused one.

Long horizons over fast closes

The renter I find a great first apartment for becomes the buyer I help five years later. A landlord whose unit I fill thoughtfully becomes the landlord who refers me to their friends. I optimize for that relationship.
